1. Introduction

1.1 Project Background

Financial markets are commonly described as systems driven by economic fundamentals and quantitative indicators. However, market performance is also significantly influenced by investor behaviour, collective expectations, and the broader information environment.

The period from 2020 to 2025 provides a particularly relevant context for examining these dynamics. Beginning with the COVID-19 pandemic, global markets experienced sharp declines, followed by recovery phases shaped by fiscal stimulus, inflationary pressures, monetary tightening, and renewed technological growth. These events were accompanied by shifts in public narratives, investor attention, and perceived risk levels.

Market volatility during this period suggests that price movements were not solely responses to fundamental economic data, but were also influenced by changing sentiment, uncertainty, and collective reactions to unfolding events. Patterns of heightened volatility, rapid sell-offs, and sustained rallies reflect the interaction between information flow and investor psychology.

This project investigates how market performance evolves alongside changes in broader informational and behavioural factors. By examining data across this five-year period, the study aims to provide insights into how collective dynamics are reflected in financial market movements.

1.2 Project Objective

The primary objective of this project is to investigate how S&P 500 market performance interacted with investor sentiment, information flow, and perceived market risk over the five-year period from 1 January 2020 to 31 December 2025. Rather than examining these forces in isolation, the project adopts an integrated analytical framework drawing on four data dimensions:

  1. S&P 500 price data — to track overall market performance and identify statistically significant movements across distinct economic phases, including the pandemic crisis, stimulus-driven recovery, inflationary tightening, and renewed growth.
  2. Financial news analysis — to quantify the tone and thematic content of market-related media coverage, and to assess how the informational environment evolves during periods of stress and stability.
  3. Google Search Trends — to capture shifts in public attention and retail investor interest, serving as a behavioural proxy for the level of awareness and anxiety surrounding financial markets at any given time.
  4. The VIX Volatility Index — to measure forward-looking implied volatility as priced by options markets, representing the aggregate market expectation of near-term price uncertainty.

By analysing these four dimensions collectively and aligning them along a common timeline, the project aims to identify patterns of co-movement, temporal leads and lags, and divergences between price action and sentiment. The overarching goal is to construct a coherent visual narrative that illustrates how financial markets respond to, and are shaped by, the dynamic interplay between quantitative market signals, media discourse, and collective investor behaviour.

3.2 Financial News Analysis

3.2.1 Purpose

The purpose of this component is to examine how financial news content evolves across different market phases between 2020 and 2025. This analysis seeks to capture both the dominant themes present in financial headlines and the overall tone of news coverage during periods of market decline, recovery, and growth.

By analysing textual data from financial news headlines, this component provides insight into the informational environment surrounding market movements and contributes to understanding how media narratives may align with investor behaviour.

3.2.2 Strengths of Original Visualization

  • Provides a visually intuitive representation of frequently occurring words in financial headlines.
  • Quickly highlights dominant themes within the dataset.
  • Engages audiences through an accessible and easily interpretable format.
  • Offers a high-level overview of recurring topics during the selected timeframe.

3.2.3 Weaknesses of Original Visualization

  • Word frequency alone does not capture the emotional tone of the headlines.
  • Prominent terms may include non-informative or generic words if preprocessing is insufficient.
  • Does not distinguish between positive, neutral, or negative narratives.
  • Lacks temporal context and does not show how tone changes over time.
  • Cannot directly illustrate relationships between news content and market performance.

As a result, the original visualization provides thematic insight but limited analytical depth regarding behavioural and sentiment dynamics.

3.2.4 Proposed Improvements

To enhance analytical clarity and relevance, this component will incorporate two complementary visualizations:

  1. Refined Word Cloud (Frequency-Based)
    Stopwords and non-informative terms will be removed through preprocessing techniques such as tokenization and text cleaning. The resulting word cloud will highlight financially meaningful and recurring terms, providing a clearer representation of dominant themes.

  2. Sentiment Score Visualization (Quantitative Analysis)
    Sentiment analysis will be performed on financial headlines to compute aggregated sentiment scores over time. These scores will be visualized using a bar chart or time-series chart to illustrate fluctuations in positive, neutral, and negative tone across different market phases.

By separating thematic prominence (word frequency) from emotional tone (sentiment scoring), the visualization framework improves interpretability and allows clearer comparison with S&P 500 movements and volatility levels.

3.3 Google Search Trends

3.3.1 Purpose

The purpose of this component is to analyse how public attention and investors interest in financial markets evolved during the covid timeframe. By examining Google Search Trends for relevant keywords, we aim to capture the correlation between search interest (types of searches and frequency) and market events.

3.3.2 Strengths of Original Visualization

  • Effectively visualizes behavioural shifts accress many topics

  • highlights sentiment spikes during covid

  • provides intuitive multi-category comparison

The original visualization effectively captures behavioral changes across multiple topics simultaneously, allowing observation of widespread shifts in public attention during the pandemic period. It highlights major sentiment changes through visible spikes and declines in search interest, particularly during periods of heightened uncertainty.

The use of multiple categories supports pattern recognition across different themes, such as consumption behavior and crisis-related concerns. Additionally, event markers add useful real-world context by linking major occurrences to sudden changes in search activity. Overall, the visualization is visually engaging, intuitive, and accessible for exploratory analysis.

3.3.3 Weaknesses of Original Visualization

  • Overwhelming amount of information

  • No direct link to financial market indicators

  • Hard to isolate pre-, during, and post-COVID phases

Despite its strengths, the original visualization presents a large volume of information simultaneously, which may overwhelm users and make focused analysis difficult. The reliance on normalized search interest values limits precise quantitative interpretation of sentiment intensity.

Furthermore, the visualization does not directly integrate financial indicators such as stock prices or volatility measures, restricting its ability to demonstrate explicit relationships between public sentiment and market movements. The continuous timeline also lacks clear segmentation of pre-COVID, during-COVID, and post-COVID phases for structured comparison

3.3.4 Proposed Improvements

  1. Show search trends similarly
  2. Tailor normal, new normal and unusual according to timeline OR change to search results which has had the least to the most effect on stock prices
  3. Clicking on search result will show the search trend during the timeline and the news headlines together with the stock price movement during the timeline. This will allow us to see the correlation between search trends, news headlines and stock price movement.
  4. Other Factors like fear index can also be added to show the correlation between all the factors.
